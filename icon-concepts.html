Brand · icon round 2

Marks that stick.

Three bolder directions, each built the way Apple, Nike and FedEx build theirs — one solid silhouette with a single hook you can't unsee. All pass the napkin test, all work in one ink colour, all hold up at favicon size.

Symbol · a bite (Apple) Motion · a swoosh (Nike) Hidden letter · negative space (FedEx)
ICON A

Cove

// the bay, bitten in — symbolic, like Apple

A single confident disc with a sheltered bay scooped out of one side, and a small vessel resting safely in the mouth of it. One solid shape, one idea: a harbour for your operations.

The hook: like Apple's bite — the curve carved out of the circle is the thing people remember. The little boat makes it unmistakably a cove, not a Pac-Man.

ICON B

Swell

// flow + done, in one stroke — abstract, like Nike

One bold, asymmetric wave that rolls up and flicks — reading at once as a wave settling into a calm bay and as a checkmark. Pure motion: work flowing smoothly, the job done.

The hook: a swoosh that's also a tick. Heavier on the left for forward momentum — the same trick that makes Nike's mark feel like it's moving.

ICON C

Keystone

// the K, hidden in the tile — negative space, like FedEx

A solid app-tile with the K cut clean out of the negative space — and its lower leg isn't straight, it curves away like the inner shore of a cove. The most app-native and "ownable" of the three.

The hook: the letter lives in the empty space, FedEx-style — and the curved leg is the cove. Reads as "K" instantly, rewards a second look.
