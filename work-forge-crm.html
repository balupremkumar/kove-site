The problem

Every off-the-shelf CRM I tried had something that made me grit my teeth. Notion was slow. HubSpot was bloated. Pipedrive looked like 2014. Monday wanted $40 per user per month for features I'd already paid for three times over.

What I needed wasn't complicated — a kanban view of leads, custom fields, a dashboard, follow-up reminders, automation when status changes. Every tool had 80% of that plus 200% of what I didn't want.

What I built

Forge — a fast, dark, kanban-first CRM that does exactly what I need and nothing more.

Core features

Multiple boards with custom stages · custom fields per board (text, number, date, select, person) · automation rules on every status change · dashboard with a 14-day activity sparkline · search across all items · drag-and-drop kanban with optimistic updates · a dark UI with emerald/amber/red signal colours · sub-1-second cold load.

Notable decisions

node:sqlite over better-sqlite3, because native compile on Node 24 was a nightmare. Optimistic UI everywhere — perceived speed is the feature. SSR for the first paint, client-side after. No build step in dev.

The result

3 SaaS subscriptions retired — Notion, Pipedrive, and a calendar tool. Under 1 second page load versus Notion's 8 for the same data. Used daily for lead pipeline (fed by the Lead Generator), project tracking and follow-ups. Total dev time: ~3 weekends.
