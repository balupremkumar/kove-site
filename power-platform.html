Straight answers

The questions that decide this.

Do we need premium Microsoft licences for this?

Often no. A lot of useful work runs on the standard Business licence you already have: SharePoint lists, standard flows, Power Apps against SharePoint data. Premium connectors, Dataverse and Copilot Studio have their own pricing, and I'll put the exact licensing cost in front of you before any build starts. No surprises on the first invoice.

SharePoint or Dataverse — which should we use?

SharePoint, until it isn't enough. For most firms under 50 staff, SharePoint lists handle the job and cost nothing extra. Dataverse earns its licence fee when you need relational depth, row-level security, or serious volume. I'll tell you which side of that line you're on. It's usually the cheap side.

How long does a Power App take to build?

A focused app on SharePoint data: one to two weeks to working software. Something bigger, with flows, approvals and an agent on top: the standard 4–8 week engagement. You see it working early and test it on real jobs while it's still easy to change.

Someone already built us flows and apps. Can you fix them?

Yes, and it's a common ask. Half-built Power Platform setups are everywhere: a flow that breaks monthly, an app one person understands. I'll audit what's there, fix what's worth fixing, and rebuild what isn't. You get documentation either way, so it never depends on one person again.

What do Copilot Studio agents actually cost to run?

Microsoft prices Copilot Studio by message volume, and for a small firm's realistic usage it's usually modest. But the honest answer is that an agent isn't always the right tool. Sometimes a plain automated flow does the job for less. I'll cost both options before you commit to either.

Is Power Apps the right answer for everything?

No. Low-code wins on speed and cost when the problem fits its shape. When it doesn't, forcing it gets expensive and slow. I write custom code as well, so my recommendation isn't bent by what I'm able to build. If custom is cheaper over three years, that's what I'll tell you.
